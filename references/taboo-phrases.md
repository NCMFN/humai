# Taboo Phrases Inventory

This inventory categorizes phrases common in AI-generated academic text that should be systematically removed or modified to sound like natural human prose.

## Category A: Always remove

These phrases function as hollow intensifiers, overused transitional constructions, or template structural crutches that humans rarely use in professional academic writing.

- "It is worth noting that"
- "It is imperative that"
- "It's not just about"
- "delve into"
- "shed light on"
- "multifaceted"
- "underscore the importance of"
- "underscoring"
- "showcasing"
- "highlighting" (when used as a participle to end a sentence without a direct object)
- "The remainder of this paper is organized as follows"
- "Due to the fact that"
- "In order to"

## Category B: Remove unless quantified

These words are used by AI to inflate the significance of an action or result. In scientific writing, claims must be specific and measurable.

- "significantly" (unless referring to statistical significance, e.g., $p < 0.05$)
- "substantially" (must be accompanied by a percentage, e.g., "by 35.8%")
- "comprehensively"
- "drastically"
- "exponentially" (unless describing actual mathematical exponentiation)

## Category C: Context-dependent

These words are perfectly acceptable when technically defined, but should be removed or substituted when used as vague filler or synonyms for "good."

- "robust" (acceptable in engineering/statistics when referring to resilience against noise/outliers; unacceptable when meaning "very good")
- "leverage" (replace with "apply," "employ," or "use" when referring to methods)
- "utilize" (replace with "use")
- "facilitate" (replace with "enable," "support," or "allow")
- "demonstrate" (replace with "show," "reveal," or "confirm" depending on the precise epistemic claim being made)
- "framework" (acceptable when formally proposing a system architecture; unacceptable as a synonym for "method")
- "paradigm" (only use when discussing actual Kuhnian paradigm shifts)
