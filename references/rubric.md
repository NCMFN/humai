# Rubric for "Human Enough" Academic Prose

This rubric defines the criteria used to evaluate whether academic prose reads as naturally authored by a human researcher, rather than an AI.

1. **Sentence length variance (measured, not estimated)**
   - Expectation: Natural rhythm is achieved by mixing short declarative sentences with longer analytical ones. The standard deviation of sentence lengths must be ≥ 5 words across each paragraph, and the target variance across the document should be 8–30 words (never uniform).

2. **Paragraph-opening diversity**
   - Expectation: No two consecutive paragraphs should begin the same way. The paragraph-opening lexical diversity must be ≥ 80% across the full document.

3. **Absence of flagged AI-signature phrases**
   - Expectation: The text must have zero instances of Category A taboo phrases. Usage of Category B and C phrases should strictly follow the rules in `taboo-phrases.md`.

4. **Fact preservation integrity**
   - Expectation: 100% preservation of all locked facts (e.g., numerical values, percentages, statistical results, author names, institutional affiliations, dataset names, dates, geographic references, version identifiers).

5. **Disciplinary register consistency**
   - Expectation: The vocabulary and tone must be calibrated to match the target venue's published style (e.g., an IEEE conference, Elsevier journal). The text must maintain a third-person academic voice unless the source is explicitly first-person.

6. **Citation and terminology accuracy**
   - Expectation: All in-text citations, reference list entries, proper nouns, and named methodologies must be preserved exactly. Citation formatting must not be altered.

7. **Logical flow without over-signposting**
   - Expectation: The text should transition smoothly between ideas without relying on template paragraph structures (e.g., "The remainder of this paper is organized as follows") or overused transitional constructions (e.g., "It is worth noting that").

8. **Absence of redundant summarization within the same section**
   - Expectation: Sections should not unnecessarily repeat or summarize what was just stated, particularly avoiding outline-like "Challenges and Future Prospects" structures unless they are specifically factual and required by the paper's organization.
