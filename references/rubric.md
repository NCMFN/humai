# Rubric: "Human Enough" Criteria

This rubric defines the 8 essential criteria for determining whether text reads as naturally human-authored rather than AI-generated. A score of 4 or higher across all categories is required.

1. **Natural Rhythm (Score 1-5):**
   - Human writing naturally varies sentence lengths. Some are very short. Others are longer and more complex. AI tends to default to medium-length, uniformly structured sentences. A score of 5 indicates a highly varied, natural cadence.

2. **Concrete vs. Abstract Verbs (Score 1-5):**
   - AI relies on abstract "copula avoidance" verbs ("serves as", "functions as", "stands as"). Humans use direct, concrete verbs ("is", "are", "does", "makes"). A score of 5 indicates complete reliance on direct, specific verbs.

3. **Absence of Template Structures (Score 1-5):**
   - AI frequently employs formulaic structures like "Despite X challenges... Y continues to thrive", the "Rule of Three" (X, Y, and Z), or negative parallelisms ("It's not just X; it's Y"). A score of 5 means zero reliance on these predictable templates.

4. **Specificity over Vague Claims (Score 1-5):**
   - AI uses "significance inflation" ("marking a pivotal moment", "in today's rapidly evolving landscape") and vague attributions ("experts believe"). Humans use exact dates, specific names, and concrete facts. A score of 5 means every claim is grounded in specificity.

5. **Elimination of "Superficial -ing" Analyses (Score 1-5):**
   - AI loves to string together participles to create an illusion of depth ("symbolizing...", "reflecting...", "showcasing...", "underscoring..."). A score of 5 means these are completely eliminated in favor of direct statements of cause and effect.

6. **Natural Formatting & Punctuation (Score 1-5):**
   - AI overuses em dashes, boldface headers inline, title case for minor headings, emojis, and curly quotes. Humans format simply. A score of 5 indicates standard, unadorned formatting and punctuation.

7. **Direct Conclusions (Score 1-5):**
   - AI ends with generic, upbeat summaries ("In conclusion, the future looks bright..."). Humans end when the point is made or with a specific forward-looking fact. A score of 5 means the conclusion is substantive and specific.

8. **Tone Appropriateness (Score 1-5):**
   - AI often adopts a sycophantic, overly enthusiastic ("Great question!"), or overly hedged ("It could potentially possibly be argued") tone. Humans write with measured confidence. A score of 5 indicates a steady, professional, yet conversational tone without filler or false enthusiasm.
