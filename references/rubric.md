# Rubric: "Human Enough" Criteria

To ensure AI-generated writing has been properly humanized, check the text against these 8 criteria:

1. **Rhythm and Variation:** Does the text vary sentence length naturally? Are there short, punchy sentences mixed with longer, more complex ones?
2. **Specific Details:** Does the text rely on specific facts, dates, names, and concrete examples rather than vague generalizations or inflated claims?
3. **Tone and Voice:** Does the text sound like a real person with a point of view, acknowledging complexity or mixed feelings, rather than neutral, sterile reporting?
4. **Simple Constructions:** Are verbs simple and direct? Does the text avoid convoluted substitutions for basic copulas like "is" or "are" (e.g., avoiding "serves as", "boasts", "features")?
5. **No AI Vocabulary:** Is the text free of overused AI buzzwords like "delve", "robust", "leverage", "testament", "tapestry", or "pivotal"?
6. **No Formulaic Structures:** Does the text avoid outline-like sections (e.g., predictable "Challenges and Future Prospects" endings), rule-of-three list forcing, and negative parallelisms ("Not only X, but Y")?
7. **Appropriate Punctuation and Formatting:** Is the text free from excessive em dashes, boldface spam, emojis, curly quotes, and title-case inline-header lists?
8. **No Chatbot Artifacts:** Are there zero remnants of conversational AI behavior, such as "I hope this helps", knowledge-cutoff disclaimers, or overly sycophantic praise?
