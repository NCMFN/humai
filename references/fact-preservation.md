# Fact Preservation Rules

This document outlines the strict rules governing what content must never be altered during the humanization process. In academic prose, intellectual rigor and data integrity must take absolute precedence over stylistic flow.

## 1. Quantitative Results

- **Numbers:** All numerical data, counts, thresholds, and measurements (e.g., 500 households, 100,000 records).
- **Percentages:** Any ratio or percentage improvement (e.g., 35.8% RMSE reduction, 4.3% increase).
- **Units:** The specific unit of measurement corresponding to any figure (e.g., kWh, °C, TWh, kW).
- **Time/Dates:** All references to time periods, hours, years, or frequency (e.g., 2021, 24-hour, 07:00–09:00).

## 2. Statistical Notation and Metrics

- **Equations/Variables:** All mathematical variables or representations (e.g., $w \approx 0.54$, $\alpha = 1.0$, $P$, $y$).
- **Metrics:** Acronyms or names representing the formal evaluation of the work (e.g., RMSE, MAE, R², MAPE).
- **p-values and Correlations:** Any statements regarding statistical significance or correlation coefficients (e.g., $r = 0.73$, $p < 0.001$).
- **Hyperparameters:** Specific algorithmic settings detailed in the methods (e.g., depth = 6, $\eta = 0.05$, leaves = 31).

## 3. Proper Nouns and Named Methodologies

- **Models/Algorithms:** Specific implementations or tools used (e.g., XGBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost, ARIMAX, TBATS, DCNN-LSTM, SHAP).
- **Datasets:** The formal names of datasets or benchmarks (e.g., UCI Household Power Consumption, Smart Home Energy dataset, SMH, HPC).
- **Preprocessing Methods:** Specific techniques applied to data (e.g., RobustScaler, TimeSeriesSplit, SLSQP, Ridge regression, forward-fill).
- **Author/Institution Details:** Names of authors, universities, and geographic locations (e.g., Udoh, Emmanuel Abraham, University of Uyo, Nigeria).

## 4. Citation Keys and Formatting

- **In-text Citations:** Never alter the format or numbering of citations (e.g., [1], [5], [9]–[12]).
- **Reference List:** The bibliography section must remain completely untouched, including all titles, authors, DOIs, URLs, and publication years.

## 5. Section and Subsection Numbering

- **Structure:** Do not alter the formal headings, Roman numerals, or alphabetic numbering (e.g., I. INTRODUCTION, A. Dataset Description).
- **Cross-references:** Any textual reference pointing to another section, table, or figure (e.g., Table I, Fig. 1) must remain exactly as written.
